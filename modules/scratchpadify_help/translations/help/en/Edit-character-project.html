• In the edit mode of a character project you see an empty matrix.
  • Below the matrix there is a line of tabs.
    • 'Filter taxa' is used to search for one taxon. As you type the list of taxa in the matrix will be reduced until you have only one taxon left.
    • 'Data editor' is where you add and edit characters and groups.
    • Under 'Organise characters' you can move around characters and groups so that they are sorted in the best way.
    • Under 'Select taxa' you can add or remove taxa.
    • Under 'Export data' you can choose different formats of data export.
    • Under 'Project settings' you can change the title of the project or delete it.
  • If you haven't selected your taxa, yet, or want to change them, click on the 'Select taxa' tab, extend the tree and select the taxa you need for this project.
  • To add characters to your matrix either click on 'Add character' in the upper right corner or click on the 'Data editor' tab.

  • To fill the matrix with data, click on a field in the matrix. You can either the data directly into the field or for text and DNA character you can enter text into the 'Edit character state' box below the matrix. You can also attribute notes to each character state.
  • If you hover with the mouse over the column number, the group and character title are shown. To adjust the width of the columns in the table click and drag the vertical double lines between the column numbers.
  • To move characters into a different position, click on 'Organise characters'. Drag and drop the groups to change their position and click the 'Save changes' button. To change the position of characters within or between groups, extend the groups by clicking on the arrow icon. You see a list of characters in the group and can drop and drag them into a different position. If a character doesn't want to move to a place at the bottom of a list of characters, try dropping it higher in the list and then move it lower or drag the lower ones up.
  • If you want to export you matrix go to 'Export data'. Select the characters you want to edit (Ctrl+click on PC, Apple+click on Mac). Select the format you need and click on 'Export'.
