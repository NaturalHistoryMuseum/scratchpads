• After you have applied for a Scratchpad and received the confirmation email you can log in with your user name and password.
  • Choose a theme for your site.
  • arrange the blocks to your liking,
  • configure your front page.
  • Personalize your account.
  • Adapt user profiles. If you add for example fields about taxonomic interest and web interest you will have a better understanding of your users and their needs. It will help you to recognize spam subscriptions and to choose a user role.
  • Add a classification.
  • Create content. To link the different content together it is best to add the bibliography first, then locations, specimens and images.

Tips:

  • Think about what kind of information you want to present on your Scratchpad. If you want a taxonomic site you will probably need to import a taxonomic classification and tag the nodes to this classification. If the site is more focussed on a social aspect you might prefer to have free tagging.
  • Think about how you want to present the information on your site. If you want to give some users access to some content and other users access to different content, you should establish groups before you create the content, so that you can link the content to a group immediately.
  • If you want to add many images you should first create image galleries to properly sort your images.

Training:

We offer basic and advanced training courses and if you don't have the opportunity to come to one of the courses, you can also do the manuals for the training courses on your own.
